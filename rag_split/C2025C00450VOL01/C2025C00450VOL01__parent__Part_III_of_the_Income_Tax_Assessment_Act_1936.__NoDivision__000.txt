Part III of the Income Tax Assessment Act 1936.

convertible securities: securities are convertible into another class 
of securities if the holder may, by the exercise of rights attached to 
those securities:
(a) have the other class of securities issued to them; or
(b) have the securities transform into securities of that other 
class.

An option may be a convertible security even if it is 
non-renounceable.
